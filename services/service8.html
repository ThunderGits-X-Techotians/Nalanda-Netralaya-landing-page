D.C.T

"D.C.T" refers to "Dynamic Contour Tonometer," a service used for precise measurement of intraocular pressure in eye care.

About our d.c.t service

At Nalanda Netralaya, our D.C.T. (Dynamic Contour Tonometer) service offers advanced, precise measurement of intraocular pressure. Utilizing cutting-edge technology, the D.C.T. provides accurate assessments without the need for eye drops, ensuring a comfortable and reliable evaluation of eye health, particularly for glaucoma management.

about D.c.t

The Dynamic Contour Tonometer (DCT) is a specialized device used in eye care to measure intraocular pressure (IOP) with high precision. Unlike traditional tonometry methods that rely on flattening the cornea, DCT adapts to the natural contour of the eye, providing more accurate readings. This method is less affected by corneal properties such as thickness and rigidity, making it particularly useful for patients with thin or irregular corneas. It is also a non-invasive and comfortable procedure, enhancing patient experience. DCT delivers consistent, repeatable results, which are crucial for monitoring IOP over time, especially in glaucoma management. By offering reliable measurements, DCT plays an important role in the early detection and effective treatment of glaucoma.

Benefits of d.c.t

The Dynamic Contour Tonometer (DCT) provides accurate intraocular pressure (IOP) readings by conforming to the eye's natural contour, making it less affected by corneal thickness or irregularities. It offers a non-invasive, comfortable experience with consistent, reliable measurements, especially useful in managing glaucoma and detecting it early.

Early Glaucoma Detection

DCT aids in detecting glaucoma at an earlier stage by providing more accurate IOP measurements, improving the chances of timely intervention.

Precision

DCT provides highly accurate IOP readings by adapting to the natural contour of the cornea, reducing the influence of corneal thickness on measurements.

Reduced Corneal Impact

Unlike conventional methods, DCT is less affected by variations in corneal properties such as thickness and rigidity, making it more reliable for patients with thin or irregular corneas.

Non-invasive and Comfortable

It is a gentle, non-invasive procedure, offering more comfort to patients compared to traditional tonometry methods like applanation.
