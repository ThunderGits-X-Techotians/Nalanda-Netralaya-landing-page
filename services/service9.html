about d.c.r

Dacryocystorhinostomy (DCR) is a surgical procedure performed to treat blocked tear ducts, which can cause excessive tearing, eye irritation, and frequent infections. The blockage typically occurs in the nasolacrimal duct, which is responsible for draining tears from the eye into the nasal cavity. During a DCR procedure, a new channel is created between the tear sac and the nasal cavity to bypass the blockage, allowing tears to drain properly. This can be done either externally, through a small incision on the side of the nose, or internally, using endoscopic techniques through the nasal passage. DCR is commonly recommended for patients who do not respond to other treatments, providing lasting relief from tear duct obstructions and improving overall eye comfort and health.

Benefits D.C.R SERVICE

Restored Tear Drainage

DCR effectively clears blocked tear ducts, allowing normal tear flow and reducing excessive tearing (epiphora).

Relief from Discomfort

The procedure alleviates symptoms like eye irritation, redness, and swelling caused by tear duct blockages.

Reduced Risk of Infections

By restoring proper tear drainage, DCR helps prevent recurring eye infections and inflammation (dacryocystitis).

Long-term Results

DCR provides a permanent solution to tear duct blockages, improving overall eye health and patient comfort.
