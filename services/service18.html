about contact lens

Contact lenses are thin, curved devices placed directly on the eye's surface to correct vision problems such as nearsightedness, farsightedness, or astigmatism. They come in various types, including soft, rigid gas permeable, and specialty lenses for specific conditions. Contacts provide a flexible and often more discreet alternative to glasses, allowing for natural vision correction and convenience. Proper fitting and care are essential to ensure comfort and eye health.
